A Legacy of Trust

Founded in 1924, The National Chronicle has stood as a pillar of democracy for over 100 years. We began as a small weekly pamphlet during the independence movement and have grown into a digital powerhouse.

Under the visionary leadership of Leela Vennu, our mission is to provide unbiased, accurate, and timely news. We do not bow to corporate interests or political pressure. Our loyalty is to the citizens.

Today, we employ over 500 fearless journalists across 20 state bureaus. From the bustling streets of Mumbai to the remote villages of the Northeast, our reporters are on the ground, bringing you stories that matter.
