Government Unveils 'Digital Bharat 2.0' to Connect 500 Districts

By Leela Vennu Updated: 10:30 AM IST

The Central Government today launched the ambitious "Digital Bharat 2.0" initiative, pledging a massive investment of ₹50,000 Crores. The primary goal is to ensure high-speed fiber optic connectivity reaches the remotest 500 districts of the country within the next 18 months.

Speaking at the press conference in New Delhi, the Minister of Technology emphasized that internet access is no longer a luxury but a fundamental right. "We are building the highways of the future," he stated. This follows the successful completion of Phase 1, which connected 200 urban centers. The project aims to bridge the digital divide that has long separated rural India from its urban counterparts.

Experts predict this will boost rural GDP by 3% within the next fiscal year by enabling e-commerce, telemedicine, and online education. However, opposition leaders have asked for a clearer roadmap regarding the timeline of implementation, citing delays in previous infrastructure projects. Despite the criticism, telecom stocks rallied by 5% in early trade as investors welcomed the news.
